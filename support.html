What to Include When Reporting an Issue

To help us investigate issues quickly, please include as much of the following information as possible:

  • Your iPhone model
  • Your iOS version
  • The SampleKing app version
  • The song or artist you were trying to identify
  • Whether music was playing from speakers, Bluetooth, or the same device
  • A brief description of what happened
  • Any error message you saw

Music Recognition Notes

SampleKing uses your device microphone to identify music playing nearby. Music recognition usually takes several seconds.

Best experience: SampleKing works best when music is playing through speakers from a nearby source.

Please keep the following in mind:

  • Recognition typically takes around 6 seconds.
  • The microphone is used temporarily during recognition and then turns off.
  • SampleKing works best when music is playing clearly through speakers.
  • If music is playing from the same phone through Bluetooth, you may hear a brief pause when recognition starts.
  • Headphones are not currently supported for recognition because the app needs to hear the music through the microphone.

Troubleshooting

The app did not recognize the song

Try moving the phone closer to the speaker, increasing the volume slightly, or reducing background noise.

No samples were found

SampleKing uses multiple music data sources to identify sample relationships. Some songs may not have available sample information, or the data may be incomplete.

Album art or metadata looks incorrect

Music metadata is compiled from third-party sources and may occasionally be incomplete or incorrect. We are continuously working to improve accuracy.

The app appears to pause briefly during recognition

If you are streaming music from the same phone through Bluetooth, the system may briefly pause or lower playback while microphone recognition begins. Playback should resume shortly after recognition starts.
